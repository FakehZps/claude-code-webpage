## The Last Ride

Toussaint is not Velen. Toussaint is not Novigrad. Toussaint is sun-soaked vineyard terraces, tournament grounds, and architecture that would embarrass Versailles. After forty hours of swamp and grey northern sky, arriving here feels like breaking through cloud cover. The contrast is the point.

> TRAVEL LOG: Duchy of Toussaint. Population: cheerful. Crime rate: surprisingly high.

## The Scope

Blood and Wine is larger than most standalone RPGs. The main quest runs 20 hours. Side content adds another 15. A full bestiary expansion. A property to manage. A knight's tournament. A fairy-tale dimension that shouldn't work and absolutely does.

The villain, Detlaff, is not Hearts of Stone-level writing — he is more archetype than character. But the mystery structure surrounding him holds, and the revelation lands with appropriate weight.

> WINE CRITIC: Dun Tynne Slope, 1269. Notes of oak, iron, and poor decisions.

## The Ending

I chose the right ending. Geralt at Corvo Bianco, tending his vineyard, Ciri visiting, the sun on stone. After everything the continent put him through — after Velen, after Novigrad, after the Isle of Mists — he gets to put the swords away.

It is not triumphant. It is quiet. It is correct.

## Final Verdict

*That we do.* Blood and Wine is the ending Geralt deserved. That CD Projekt Red delivered it, in an expansion, a decade ago, remains remarkable.
