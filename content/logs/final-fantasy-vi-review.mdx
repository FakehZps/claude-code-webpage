## Loading Save State

Final Fantasy VI carries the weight of legend. Ask any JRPG veteran and they'll tell you it's the pinnacle — Kefka as the greatest villain, the opera scene as the greatest moment, the ensemble cast as the greatest achievement in 16-bit storytelling. I went in expecting to have my mind rewritten.

> SYSTEM LOG: Expectations set to maximum. Disappointment loading…

It didn't happen.

## What Works

The sprite work is genuinely extraordinary. The pixel art holds up in a way that Dragon Quest IV's does not — there's expressiveness in these tiny characters that modern HD-2D games are still trying to replicate. Kefka is unhinged in a way that feels genuinely menacing, not campy. The world-of-ruin pivot is structurally bold.

> ESPER TRANSMISSION: Magic granted. Purpose unclear.

The soundtrack needs no defence. Terra's Theme is one of the finest pieces of game music ever written and I will not hear otherwise.

## What Doesn't

The combat is a slot machine dressed in ATB clothing. Vanish/Doom trivialities aside, there's little strategic depth to push back on. The ensemble cast — celebrated for its size — means almost no one gets enough screen time to matter. Celes and Terra are compelling; Gau is a mechanic disguised as a character.

The Switch port adds nothing and smooths over nothing.

## Final Verdict

*Not up to the hype.* A formative artefact I respect without loving. Sometimes the classics stay classics because no one dares say otherwise.
