## The Offer

Gaunter O'Dimm walks into the story like he owns it. By the end of Hearts of Stone, you understand that he does. CD Projekt Red took the base game's sprawling structure and compressed it into something almost novella-tight — a contained arc about deals, regret, and the particular arrogance of men who believe they can outwit the inevitable.

## Olgierd

The expansion's villain-protagonist is the best character in the Witcher universe, and I include Geralt in that assessment. Von Everec is not a straightforward monster. He is a man who got everything he wanted and discovered the cost too late. His three impossible tasks are not padding — they are character study.

The Wedding sequence alone is worth the price. It is absurdist, melancholy, and somehow affecting. A ghost who just wants to dance with his wife one more time. There is nothing cyberpunk about it and nothing about it that feels out of place here.

## The Condensed Form

At roughly 10 hours, Hearts of Stone is a better-paced experience than the base game. Every scene earns its place. The twist with O'Dimm lands because the expansion respects the player's intelligence enough not to telegraph it.

## Final Verdict

*Another contract, another coin.* The finest single piece of Witcher content ever made — and one of the finest pieces of RPG DLC, full stop.
