## Wanted Level: Active

Star Wars: Outlaws had a rough launch. Reviews were mixed. The stealth was labelled clunky, the open world labelled empty, the protagonist labelled derivative. A year of patches later, the game I played on PS5 is something different — tight enough to recommend, confident enough to love in patches.

> SYNDICATE ALERT: Hutt Cartel reputation declining. Jabba is displeased.

## Kay Vess

She works. Not because she subverts the rogue archetype — she doesn't — but because Ubisoft Massive committed to the bit. Kay is sarcastic, self-interested, and gradually, convincingly, becomes someone you root for. Nix the companion creature is the best implementation of an animal sidekick in the genre since Dogmeat.

The faction reputation system is the mechanical spine of the game. Balancing favour between the Hutt Cartel, the Crimson Dawn, and the Pike Syndicate while running jobs for all three creates genuine tension. Choices have syndicate-wide consequences. Getting locked out of a vendor because you robbed the wrong safe three hours ago is exactly the kind of systemic friction this genre needs.

> KAY VESS: "We're getting paid for this, right?" ROGUE: "Eventually."

## The Galaxy

The planets are smaller than Ubisoft's usual scale and better for it. Toshara is a standout — a windswept moon with savannahs, criminal infrastructure, and sightlines that make the speeder bike traversal genuinely pleasurable. Kijimi and Akiva fill out the roster with enough variety to keep the loop from flattening.

The Kessel sequence is the best set-piece in any Star Wars game since Fallen Order's Zeffo.

## Final Verdict

*Dank farrik.* A scoundrel game that understands scoundrels. Not flawless. Absolutely worth it.
