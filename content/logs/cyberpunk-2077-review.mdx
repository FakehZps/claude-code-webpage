## Boot Sequence Complete

Night City does not forgive. It never promised to. But somewhere between the 2.0 overhaul and the Dogtown expansion, CDPR quietly delivered the game we were always supposed to get. I clocked 60 hours across two playthroughs before I felt ready to write this.

> SYSTEM LOG: Entry 4471 — V still standing. Night City count: zero.

The Phantom Liberty expansion adds the Dogtown district, a spy-thriller storyline starring Idris Elba as Solomon Reed, and a genuine second ending that recontextualises everything the base game built. It is, without exaggeration, the best single piece of DLC I've played this generation.

## What Changed in 2.0

The skill tree was gutted and rebuilt from scratch. Cyberware now costs RAM as well as Eurodollars. The police system actually works — Trauma Team will *hunt you down* in a convincing way. These aren't patches. They're architectural changes that make the base game feel like a different product.

> RIPPERDOC BROADCAST: Militech cyberware detected. Compliance recommended.

**Combat** feels kinetic in a way it never did at launch. Mantis Blades carry weight now. Sandy runs feel fast and consequential. I burned 15 hours just experimenting with builds I'd have ignored in 2020.

## The Story Holds

Reed and V have a believable dynamic — two people who are burning from opposite ends of the same fuse. The branching choices don't feel like A/B switches. They feel like consequences. The ending I chose left me staring at the loading screen longer than I'm willing to admit.

*Phantom Liberty doesn't erase the past. It earns the future.*

## Final Verdict

Night City was always beautiful. Now it's worth living in.
