## MEMORY_DUMP :: 2023

Another orbit complete. The drive is full. Time to defrag.

> SYSTEM: 365 days logged. 47 games initiated. 23 completed. 8 abandoned. Memory integrity: 94%.

2023 was the year the industry couldn't stop announcing layoffs while simultaneously shipping some of the best games in recent memory. The dissonance was deafening. I chose to log it all anyway.

## GOTY :: Baldur's Gate 3

No contest. Larian shipped the impossible and made it look effortless. Every other GOTY list is wrong if BG3 isn't at the top.

**Runner-up:** Alan Wake 2 — Remedy did something genuinely weird and committed to it completely.

## WORST :: Redfall

> ERROR: Co-op functionality offline. Enemy AI unresponsive. Immersion: null.

Arkane Austin deserved better. The studio that made Dishonored and Prey shipped something that felt like a vertical slice that accidentally got a release date. The real tragedy isn't the bad game — it's the talent that had to put their name on it.

## The Ones That Stuck

*Lies of P* proved the FromSoftware formula can survive in other hands. *Hi-Fi Rush* was the surprise of the year — a rhythm-action game that commits so fully to its own joy that it becomes infectious. *Armored Core VI* reminded me that FROM can still make things that aren't Souls.

## What I'm Carrying Into 2024

The backlog is a lie. I'm not catching up. I'm just making peace with the fact that some games will always be unplayed, and the ones I did reach were worth every hour logged.

*End of year. Begin new cycle.*
