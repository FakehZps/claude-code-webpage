## Roll for Initiative

I went in sceptical. I came out 140 hours later questioning every other RPG I've ever loved. Baldur's Gate 3 is the kind of game that only exists when a studio is given unlimited time, money, and ambition and told to *ship when it's ready*.

> PARTY STATUS: Shadowheart disapproves. Astarion is delighted. Gale is hungry again.

Larian built a world that reacts to you at a granular level that most open-world games haven't figured out. Your race, class, background, and choices in Act 1 ripple outward in ways you won't fully comprehend until Act 3.

## The Writing Is the Feature

Every companion has a full arc. Every minor character has a reason to exist. The villain has a coherent ideology that is *genuinely difficult to argue against* in the early hours. I had to remind myself these were not real people on two separate occasions.

> MIND-FLAYER COLONY RECORD: Subject displays resistance to assimilation. Probability of success: 12%. We proceed anyway.

**Karlach** remains the greatest character written in a video game in the last decade. Her entire storyline is a meditation on what it means to keep going when everything is stacked against you. I completed her ending and sat in silence for about four minutes.

## Combat Depth

The turn-based D&D 5e system rewards creativity over optimisation. You can win fights by shoving enemies off cliffs, dousing them in grease and igniting it, or simply talking your way past the encounter entirely. Three playthroughs in and I'm still discovering interactions I missed.

*Some games set benchmarks. BG3 moved the goalposts.*

## Final Verdict

The dice gods were kind. This is the RPG I'll be measuring everything else against for the rest of the decade.
